I wanted a title that sounded solid and foundational, so I started brainstorming concepts for a fake mineral. By combining the word "faux" (meaning fake) with the classic geological suffix "-ite" (as seen in stones like granite or diorite), I landed on _Fauxnite_. It perfectly captures the synthetic, constructed nature of a digital font.

## The Monospace Philosophy

Because I spend so much time writing code, designing a monospace font was a natural choice. Not only is the uniform character width highly functional for programming environments, but I also find the structured, grid-like alignment of monospace to be generally more aesthetically pleasing.

When it came to drafting the actual characters, I adhered to a strict and simple design philosophy by dividing the vertical workspace into horizontal thirds:

- **Top Third:** Reserved exclusively for the ascenders of uppercase letters.

- **Middle Third:** The core section shared by the bodies of both uppercase and lowercase letters.

- **Bottom Third:** Reserved exclusively for the descenders of lowercase letters.
